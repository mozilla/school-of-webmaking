1. Sign Up

How?

Click the big signup button on the module you're most interested in!

Why?

Let Mozilla know you're interested in this work and get announcements or materials via email.

2. Read and Make

How?

Click the Module Title you're interested in (Exploring, Building, Facilitating or Connecting?). By default, you'll land on the "Concepts" page, this is the reading for the module you're looking at. Click "Make" to see options for things you can make.

Why?

The articles are introductions to the themes that underpin teaching the web with participatory and collaborative methodologies, and the Make options are ways to think about these themes while gaining technical and creative skills. Each module has a different focus:

Exploring

is about the pedagogies we use in this open community

Building

will help you make resources that integrate web literacy competencies with other topics you're teaching

Facilitating

will help you use participatory and collaborative methods to make learning relevant and fun

Connecting

with the larger Mozilla community helps make your actions part of a global story

3. Discuss

How?

Scroll to the bottom of any "Concepts" or "Make" page and click the link to "Discussion".

Why?

Participating is learning. How much you dig in is entirely up to you, but the more people you try to talk to, the more you'll learn.

4. Share

How?

Post things you make or write into the Discussion area, share via Facebook, Twitter, Instagram where ever, and tag things with #teachtheweb.

Why?

The more you share with the community, the more the community will share with you, thus the more you'll learn. The more engaged you are, the more comfortable others will feel about getting engaged.

5. Join us in real time

How?

Every week we have a Community call where we invite you to share your thoughts. We have a schedule of synchronous actions here: LINK

Why?

You can get real time help, share your thoughts and join a conversation
